The Plot

Set in 1950s Italy, amidst the harrowing backdrop of the War, a palpable spirit of change was in the air.
Adriano Olivetti, a world renowned industrialist and innovator, had a dream of placing Italy at the forefront of global technology next to international giants like General Electric and IBM.

Olivetti’s concept merged form and function, leading him on a singular journey in pursuit of creating the first Italian computer, making it one of only a few in the entire world. In this unprecedented mission, he sought out ten of the brightest young engineers in Italy to bring his vision to life.

This is the story of one of those virtuoso engineers.

Giorgio Sacerdoti was weighed down by a tormented life and an acute case of polio that paralysed both of his legs. Invited to Olivetti’s small Tuscan villa, in the outskirts of Pisa, he and ten other engineers were tasked with building an electronic computer, the ultimate frontier in technical engineering at the time. The task was so pioneering that there were no tools, manuals, or framework to achieve it. So, the ten engineers started to experiment.

Days turned into nights, and weeks into months. They spent almost a year building their “machine” but, at the last minute, just before a presentation to the nation, they decided to take the entire apparatus apart.

An idea had sparked that would - almost by accident - bring them to the creation of the world’s first transistorised computer. One that would open the doors to the modern computer, the microchip, and the information technology as we know it today.

On the surface, Giorgio’s story is one of numbers and technology but underneath that is a complex artist driven by unwavering passion and curiosity, yet held back by his own profound doubt. By piecing together a narrative from Giorgio’s diaries, notes, and letters, we form an intimate glimpse of a man who is as brilliant as he is fragile; bound to his crutches and the uncompromising passage of time.

In his unrelenting quest to leave a mark on the world, Giorgio’s obsession with the battle of Time brings it to life as a character in Il Mestiere del Tempo as a visceral, unavoidable presence to be confronted and challenged.

Director Paolo Sacerdoti’s connection to the story of Il Mestiere del Tempo goes beyond his many years of immersive design and theatre expertise - he is the grand-nephew of Giorgio Sacerdoti, and it was he who meticulously pieced together Giorgio’s notes to weave this sprawling narrative that exists beyond the bounds of time.

Ultimately, Il Mestiere del Tempo is more than a portrait of a brilliant artist engineer, unravelled by his equally brilliant descendant.

It’s a deeply universal story of the inherent fragility and overwhelming beauty of the human spirit when faced with its own mortality. As we globally face an unprecedented age of uncertainty, change, and rapid technological advancement, this battle is just as relevant today as it was then.
